# Wrap-Up (Sampling Distributions)

As mentioned in the introduction, this last module in probability is the bridge between the probability section and inference. It focuses on the relationship between sample values (*statistics*) and population values (*parameters*). Statistics vary from sample to sample due to *sampling variability*, and therefore can be regarded as *random variables* whose distribution we call *sampling distribution*. In this module we focused on two statistics, the *sample proportion*, $\hat{p}$, and the *sample mean*, $\bar{X}$. Our goal was to explore the sampling distribution of these two statistics relative to their respective population parameters (p and $\mu$), and we found in *both* cases that under certain conditions the *sampling distribution is approximately normal*. This result is known as the *Central Limit Theorem.* As we'll see in the next section, the Central Limit Theorem is the foundation for statistical inference.
